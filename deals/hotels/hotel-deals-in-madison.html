Madison Features

Wisconsin’s second-largest city, Madison is a destination unto itself, sitting on the shores of Lakes Mendota, Monona, Waubesa, and Kegonsa. With its downtown set on an isthmus, it offers panoramic views of its many waterways from all over the city. With its clever location and charming features, Madison is a destination worth exploring. One of the major draws to the city is the University of Wisconsin – Madison, home of the UW Badgers. The University is a local institution, and draws alumni and sports fans from across the nation to Madison throughout the year.
With the University comes a charming downtown area, with the main thoroughfare of State Street, a primarily pedestrian area known for its cafes, unique restaurants, and boutique shopping. In the warmer months, the area also hosts the Dane County Farmers’ Market, as well as a number of massive festivals such as Rhythms and Booms, that attract visitors from all over the Midwest. Madison’s four lakes offer year-round water enjoyment, with boating and swimming in the summer, and ice skating, ice fishing and hockey in the winter. For those who opt for culture instead of nature, Madison is also home to a thriving music scene, and regularly hosts independent as well as internationally-renowned acts. The University is also a draw for the intellectual crowd, regularly putting on lectures and conferences.
With its mixture of small-town charm and big city attractions, Madison is a wonderful destination for business or pleasure, and city worth exploring.

Getting Around in Madison

Madison is connected to Wisconsin and much of the Midwest by railway and bus systems, and is easily accessible by multiple freeways. The city is very walkable, and is also extensively serviced by the Madison Metro busses and several taxi companies.

Hotel Deals in Madison

Discount Hotels

The GrandStay Residential Suites Madison offers travelers to the city a homey place to stay, whether it’s for a night or a month. This all-suite hotel is a well-kept, fully outfitted property perfect for those looking for their own space while still reaping the benefits that come standard with a hotel.
Located just outside of the downtown area, the Magnuson Grand Hotel is an ideal marriage of comfortable accommodations and price consciousness. Offering full amenities and guest packages meant to highlight the area, this hotel deal is a great stay for those who want to experience the best Madison has to offer.

Mid-Range Hotels

The Madison Concourse Hotel sits in the heart of downtown, offering guests sumptuous accommodations in the middle of everything the city has to offer. With great proximity to the University as well as the convention center, a charming desk staff, and a number of guest packages to customize your stay, this hotel has something for everyone.
The Dahlmann Campus Inn and Chancellor’s Club is a Madison institution, set just to the east of the University and within walking distance great shopping and dining. Known for their exceptional service and rich décor, this hotel makes for an elegant stay, regardless of whether you’re there for business or pleasure.

Luxury Hotels

HotelRED is Madison’s premier boutique hotel, offering ultra-modern accommodations with sleek décor, delicious dining, and spacious rooms. It is just steps away from downtown, and fully equipped with a tapas restaurant, luxurious suites, and stunning, stylish guestrooms perfect for the jet-setter hiding in anyone.
The Mansion Hill Inn is a rich, decadent hotel indicative of Madison’s dynamic history. This elegant retreat is set in a European-style mansion with striking architecture and bundles of artistic touches. Modern guestrooms and an unparalleled commitment to customer service make this one of the best stays in the state.
