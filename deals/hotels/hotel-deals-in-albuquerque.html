Albuquerque Features

Albuquerque, located in the center of New Mexico, has many of the amenities of a larger city. Its climate is comfortable, with a mix of sunshine and snow in the winter and sunshine with some rain in spring and summer months. Because of its location, you can easily drive and reach the mountains to go skiing or camping. Expose your family to one of New Mexico’s cultures and visit one of several pueblos, located within two or three hours.
The Downtown area of Albuquerque is full of museums, and you can drive west of the city to tour the Painted Dunes. The University of New Mexico is located near the downtown area, offering an eclectic mix of shops, bookstores, coffee shops and restaurants. At the end of the day, look for budget hotels in Albuquerque.
Go to one of the two malls in Albuquerque when you need to take care of shopping or buy gifts for family back home. Depending on your family’s taste in meals, choose a restaurant like the Artichoke Cafe, Los Equipales, the Nob Hill Bar & Grill, or the Rancher’s Club of New Mexico. These restaurants offer American, Mexican and steakhouse meals. Fine dining is also available at the Zinc Wine Bar & Bistro or the St. Clair Winery & Bistro.

Getting Around in Albuquerque

Albuquerque is a spread-out city with two intersecting interstates – 25 and 40, which means you need to find an easy way to get around the city. The New Mexico Rail Runner allows you to travel outside the city to Santa Fe, Bernalillo, Los Lunas and Belen. Rent a car equipped with a GPS unit and tour the city this way. Once you have oriented yourself, use the Interstates and landmarks to help you get from point A to Point B.

Hotel Deals in Albuquerque

Discount Hotels

The Comfort Inn Airport, located about 1 1/2 miles away from the Albuquerque International Sunport, offers its guests an easy budget and a convenient location. A complimentary airport shuttle, free deluxe continental breakfast, Jacuzzi and seasonal outdoor pool are all available.
Long-term visitors needing hotel deals in Albuquerque can stay at the Crossland Albuquerque Northeast, which features spacious, clean and comfortable guest suites. Each suite is equipped with full kitchens, Wi-Fi access, workspace and personalized voicemail. You can take advantage of the laundry facilities.

Mid-Range Hotels

If you are looking for something a little more upscale, book a room at the Albuquerque Marriott, located near Nob Hill. Each guest room is appointed in traditional American and furnished with armoires, walk-in closets and crystal lamps. Rooms on the higher floors come with picturesque views of the Sandia Mountains or the mesas surrounding Albuquerque.
The Albuquerque Marriott Pyramid, so named for its eye-catching construction, is located right off of Interstate 25, giving you easy access to communities north and south of the city. This hotel is the most upscale of the mid-range hotels in the city. Appointed in country-French decor, each room features sponge-painted walls.

Luxury Hotels

Hotel Albuquerque at Old Town is a combination of two of New Mexico's cultures. Suites are furnished with carved hardwood furniture and some rooms are equipped with private balconies and breathtaking views of the Sandia Mountains. This hotel is situated by the historic Old Town Plaza.
Andaluz offers 107 guest rooms and is one of the oldest buildings in Albuquerque. Each suite is designed with an earth-tone decor, original artwork and Moorish arches. Look for a central living room in the hotel with a library and in-house cultural center. Lucia, the on-site restaurant, specializes in and serves Mediterranean food. Relax with a drink at the rooftop bar and lounge, Ibiza.
